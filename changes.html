1955 - 1976





Hollywood filmmakers during The Changes Era produced more contemporary and innovative films by increasing focus on storylines with twist
endings, and blurred lines between the protagonists and antagonists. Foreign films began to appear in cinemas throughout the United States
and the spreading of televisions during this time meant audiences had more options to view films.

The Stars of the Era;
Cary Grant, Frank Sinatra, Marlon Brando!

Towards the end of The Changes Era, Hollywood explored more mature themes in their films that ultimately formed the Movie Picture Association of America (MPAA). The MPAA is an organisation representing the five major film studios (Universal Pictures, Paramount Pictures, Warner Brothers Pictures, Walt Disney Pictures, and Columbia Pictures) and the Netflix streaming service to rate the suitability for certain audiences, enable the international distribution of films for the studios, and work to prevent piracy.
